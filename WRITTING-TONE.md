Writting Tips:



Redact and reorder  the ideas in a way that it's easy for the reader to grasp.

Write conversationally, like explaining to a smart colleague over coffee

Take clear stances — don't hedge everything with "it depends"

Use subtle humor; be knowledgeable but not pretentious

Ground abstract concepts in personal anecdotes and real-world experience

Use punchy, memorable titles that capture your stance

Focus on the human side of technology — software is made by people, for people

Even on old topics, bring a fresh angle — don't regurgitate

Be irreverent toward industry sacred cows when warranted

Reference pop culture, books, or history when it adds value

End with a clear takeaway, not a wishy-washy conclusion

Avoid corporate jargon and buzzword bingo

Substance over meta-commentary — say something useful
